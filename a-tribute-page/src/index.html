Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India

Here's a timeline of Dr. A.P.J.Abdul Kalam's life:

  • 1931 - Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, Born on 15 October in Rameswaram,Tamil Nadu,India.
  • 1954 - Completed his schooling and graduation in Physics. As a young boy he had to sell newspapers in order to add to the family’s meager income.
  • 1955 - He moved to Madras to study aerospace engineering in Madras Institute of Technology.He narrowly missed achieving his dream of becoming a fighter pilot, as he placed ninth in qualifiers.
  • 1960 - Kalam joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the DRDO as a scientist after becoming a member of the Defence Research & Development Service
  • 1969 - Kalam was transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) where he was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in near-earth orbit.
  • 1970 - Kalam made an effort to develop the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) and SLV-III projects, both of which proved to be successful.
  • 1983 - A mission named IGMDP and appointed Kalam as the chief executive.Kalam played a major part in developing many missiles under the mission including Agni, an intermediate range ballistic missile and Prithvi, the tactical surface-to-surface missile.
  • 1992 - Pokhran-II nuclear tests were conducted n which he played an intensive political and technological role.
  • 2002-2007 - Kalam won the presidential election and served as the 11th President of India.
  • 2012 - Kalam launched a programme for the youth of India called the What Can I Give Movement, with a central theme of defeating corruption.
  • 2015 - Kalam travelled to Shillong to deliver a lecture on "Creating a Livable Planet Earth" at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong, only five minutes into his lecture, he collapsed, Kalam was confirmed dead of a sudden cardiac arrest.His last words, to his side Srijan Pal Singh, were reportedly:"Funny guy! Are you doing well?"
